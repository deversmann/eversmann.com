Containment and equipment

  • Door signage is accurate and legible.
  • Doors are self-closing and lockable.
  • Access to lab is limited or restricted when work with biohazardous materials is in progress.
  • PPE appropriate for the biological agent and task is utilized when working with hazardous materials.
  • Laboratory has negative airflow with respect to public areas.
  • Laboratory windows have screens or do not open.
  • Laboratory furniture is sturdy and has non-porous work surfaces.
  • Vacuum lines have backflow protection (in-line filter and secondary flask if aspirating liquid).
  • Potentially infectious materials and rDNA organisms are placed in an appropriately labeled container that prevents release to the environment during collection, handling, processing, storage or transport.
  • Biological Safety Cabinets are certified annually (list room number, serial number and certification date)

Sanitation, Disinfection and Waste

  • Sink for hand washing is present.
  • Eyewash is available, accessible and regularly flushed. (see SafetyNet #66)
  • Chemical Spill SafetyNet #13 is posted in laboratory and placed in chemical spill kit.
  • Biological Spill SafetyNet #127 is posted in laboratory and placed in biological spill kit.
  • [] Biological [] Chemical Spill kits are available and stocked with appropriate items.
  • Work surfaces are cleaned daily with the appropriate disinfectants, after each use, and after any spill of infectious materials.

Medical Waste:(No medical waste generated)

  • Lid on secondary container is securable and appropriately labeled (i.e. sides and top).
  • Waste container(s) is monitored daily and kept clean and closed when not in use.
  • Waste container is labeled with the biohazard symbol on the top and on all sides or 3-4 side areas.
  • Waste does not exceed 7 days (after start of accumulation) in the lab before transfer to medical waste accumulation site.
  • Waste is labeled with: PI, room number, building, and phone # and sealed before transport to accumulation site in a secondary container.
